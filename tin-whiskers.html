Tin Whiskers

Tiny crystalline filaments that grow spontaneously from tin surfaces. They cause short circuits, and they're the reason space and military electronics still use leaded solder.

What Are They

Tin whiskers are single-crystal filaments of pure tin that grow from tin-plated surfaces and tin-rich solder. They're not dendrites (which grow from electrical migration) — whiskers grow from compressive mechanical stress in the tin, with no applied voltage required.

PropertyTypical Value
Diameter1-5 μm
Length1 μm to 10+ mm (documented cases over 10mm)
Growth rateVariable — can grow mm per year, or sit dormant for years then suddenly sprout
StructureSingle crystal of pure β-tin
ElectricalFully conductive — a whisker bridging two traces is a dead short
Current capacityCan carry 10-50 mA before fusing (like a tiny fuse wire)

A 1 μm diameter tin whisker across a 0.5mm gap between two traces is an intermittent or permanent short circuit. In high-impedance circuits (like CMOS gates), even momentary contact can cause a latchup or logic error.

The Plasma Cascade

The really catastrophic failure isn't the short itself — it's what happens next. When a whisker bridges two conductors at higher voltages (>12V):

  1. The whisker carries current, heats up, and vaporizes — like a fuse blowing
  2. The vaporized metal creates a plasma arc in the gap
  3. The plasma is conductive — it sustains the arc even after the whisker is gone
  4. The arc carbonizes nearby PCB substrate (FR4), flux residue, and conformal coating
  5. Carbonized material is permanently conductive — the short circuit is now self-sustaining
  6. Adjacent traces and components get pulled into the arc — cascading failure

The end state: a charred crater on the board where a power rail used to be. This is how a 1 μm whisker destroys an entire subsystem. In satellite and avionics power buses (28V+), a single whisker can cause a sustained arc that takes out the whole board.

Metal vapor arc vs simple short

Below ~12V, whiskers typically just fuse open harmlessly or create intermittent logic glitches. Above ~12V, there's enough energy to sustain a metal vapor arc after the whisker vaporizes. This is why the risk severity depends heavily on the voltage domain — a 3.3V logic board has a very different whisker risk profile than a 48V power converter or a 270V aircraft power bus.

Why They Grow

The driving force is compressive stress in the tin layer. Sources of stress:

  • Substrate stress — tin plated directly on copper develops stress as copper diffuses into the tin, forming Cu₆Sn₅ intermetallic at the interface. This IMC takes up more volume than the original tin, compressing the surrounding tin.
  • Mechanical stress — bent leads, press-fit connections, thermal cycling
  • Thermal cycling — CTE mismatch between tin and the substrate creates cyclic stress
  • Corrosion — oxide formation on the tin surface can drive whisker growth
  • Electrochemical effects — in humid environments, corrosion products add stress

The whisker grows because it's the tin's way of relieving compressive stress — extruding material out from the grain boundary. It's a diffusion-driven creep process, not a surface phenomenon.

Why Lead Prevents Whiskers

Adding just 2-3% lead to tin dramatically suppresses whisker growth. The mechanism:

  • Lead atoms segregate to grain boundaries in the tin
  • This disrupts the grain boundary diffusion pathway that whiskers use to grow
  • Lead also changes the stress relaxation behavior — the tin can relieve stress by bulk creep instead of whisker extrusion
  • Additionally, lead softens the tin, making it more ductile and less prone to storing compressive stress

This is why SnPb (63/37 or 60/40) solder has essentially zero whisker risk, while pure tin or tin-rich lead-free alloys have measurable risk.

Famous Failures

Tin whiskers have caused documented failures across industries:

IncidentYearWhat Happened
Galaxy IV satellite1998Tin whiskers shorted the satellite's control processor. 80% of US pager traffic went offline for days. Estimated $400M+ in losses.
Millstone Nuclear Power Plant2005Tin whiskers caused a false alarm that led to an unnecessary shutdown. NRC investigated.
Swatch Group recalls2006Whisker-induced failures in lead-free soldered relay contacts.
Toyota accelerator recall2009While primarily a mechanical/software issue, tin whiskers on the pedal sensor PCB were investigated as a contributing factor.
F-15 radar systemsMultipleUSAF documented tin whisker failures in upgraded avionics that had switched to lead-free components.
Medical pacemakersOngoingReason pacemakers are explicitly exempt from RoHS — tin whisker failure is potentially lethal.
The scale of the problem

NASA maintains a Tin Whisker database with thousands of documented incidents: nepp.nasa.gov/whisker/. The problem isn't theoretical — it's an ongoing, active failure mode in any system using lead-free tin finishes.

RoHS Exemptions

The EU RoHS directive (Restriction of Hazardous Substances) banned lead in most electronics. But critical applications got permanent or renewable exemptions:

ExemptionWhy
Aerospace / space hardwareTin whiskers in orbit = mission failure. No rework possible.
Military weapon systemsReliability requirements incompatible with whisker risk.
Medical implants (pacemakers, defibrillators)Failure = death. Explicitly excluded from RoHS.
Automotive safety-critical (some)ABS, airbag controllers. Failure = accident.
Infrastructure (servers, telecom backbone)Renewable exemptions for high-reliability server solder.
Monitoring and control instrumentsIndustrial process control where failure has safety consequences.

For these applications, SnPb solder (typically Sn63/Pb37) continues to be used, and IPC J-STD-001 Space Addendum (ES) requires leaded solder.

Mitigation for Lead-Free

If you must use lead-free (consumer products under RoHS), here's how to reduce whisker risk:

StrategyEffectivenessNotes
Conformal coatingHighAcrylic, urethane, or epoxy coating physically restrains whiskers. Most effective single mitigation. Coating must be >50 μm thick.
Matte tin vs bright tinModerate-highMatte tin (larger grains) has significantly lower whisker propensity than bright tin (fine grains). Always specify matte tin finish on components.
Nickel underplateModerate-highNi barrier between copper and tin prevents Cu diffusion into tin, reducing the primary stress driver.
AnnealingModerateBaking tin-plated parts at 150°C for 1-2 hours relieves compressive stress. Temporary — stress can rebuild.
SAC alloy vs pure tinLow-moderateSAC305 has lower whisker risk than pure tin, but not zero. The silver and copper additions help but don't eliminate the mechanism.
Increased tin thicknessLowThicker tin (>10 μm) dilutes the compressive stress. But whiskers can still grow from thick layers.
Component selectionHighChoose components with NiPdAu or tin-bismuth finish over pure tin where available.

For Hobbyists: Should You Worry?

Probably not

Tin whiskers typically take months to years to grow to lengths that cause shorts. For hobby projects, prototypes, and consumer products with a ~5 year expected life, the risk is very low. The people who worry about whiskers are building satellites, medical implants, and industrial systems expected to run for 20+ years in harsh environments.

That said: if you're building something safety-critical or long-life, understand this failure mode. And now you know why the space industry uses leaded solder — it's not laziness, it's physics.
